# Project Charter  
## Laptop Price Analytics & Value Modeling Framework

---

## 1. Background

Laptop pricing varies significantly across brands and configurations.  
While hardware specifications are visible to consumers, the relationship between technical attributes and market pricing is often unclear.

This project was initiated to systematically analyze the factors that influence laptop pricing and to develop a structured analytical framework for evaluating value efficiency across configurations.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

There is limited transparency in understanding:

- Which hardware specifications most strongly drive price.
- How configurations differ across budget and premium segments.
- Whether higher price consistently reflects higher performance value.
- How to objectively compare configurations under budget constraints.

Without structured analysis, purchasing or pricing decisions may rely on incomplete or subjective evaluation.

---

## 3. Project Purpose

The purpose of this project is to:

1. Build a structured data transformation pipeline.
2. Engineer performance-based hardware features.
3. Develop statistical models explaining price behavior.
4. Segment laptops into meaningful performance-price clusters.
5. Create a composite scoring framework to rank configurations.
6. Generate business-level insights for informed decision-making.

---

## 4. Objectives

### Primary Objectives

- Identify key price drivers using statistical modeling.
- Quantify the relationship between hardware tiers and market price.
- Design a composite specification score for value ranking.
- Enable budget-based decision scenarios.

### Secondary Objectives

- Demonstrate structured data governance.
- Maintain reproducible transformation logic.
- Deliver executive-ready reporting outputs.

---

## 5. Scope

### In Scope

- Structured transformation of raw dataset
- SQL-based parsing and validation
- Feature engineering for modeling
- Regression analysis
- Clustering analysis
- Value scoring methodology
- Business scenario modeling
- Executive insights and reporting

### Out of Scope

- Real-time deployment
- Live pricing feeds
- External benchmarking datasets
- Time-series trend modeling
- Consumer behavior modeling

---

## 6. Success Criteria

The project will be considered successful if:

- A clean and reproducible dataset is produced.
- Statistical models identify significant price drivers.
- Meaningful performance-price clusters are formed.
- A defensible composite value scoring framework is implemented.
- Clear business insights are delivered.

---

## 7. Key Deliverables

- Structured and validated dataset
- Model-ready dataset
- Statistical modeling notebooks
- Clustering analysis
- Composite value scoring framework
- Executive summary report
- Technical documentation

---

## 8. Assumptions

- The dataset is reasonably representative of market offerings.
- Hardware tiers reflect meaningful performance hierarchy.
- Price reflects market positioning.
- All transformations are traceable and documented.

---

## 9. Constraints

- Static dataset
- No external enrichment
- Limited sample size
- No longitudinal pricing data
